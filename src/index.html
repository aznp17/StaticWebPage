Our Mission

The Science National Honor Society encourages participation in scientific and intellectual thought while advancing students' knowledge of classical and modern science.

  • Encourage scientific and intellectual thought
  • Advance knowledge of classical and modern science
  • Communicate with the scientific community
  • Aid civic community comprehension of science
  • Promote community service dedication
  • Pursue scientific knowledge benefiting mankind
🏆

Membership Benefits

SNHS membership provides prestigious recognition and demonstrates the qualities that colleges and universities seek in applicants.

  • Demonstrates excellent character to colleges
  • Shows high academic achievement
  • Proves teacher trust and recommendation
  • Official SNHS medal and graduation cord
  • Free SNHS t-shirt
  • Enhanced college application profile
🌟

Character Excellence

We seek students who exemplify the highest standards of character, integrity, and commitment to scientific principles and community service.

  • Outstanding academic performance
  • Strong moral character and integrity
  • Leadership potential and initiative
  • Commitment to community service
  • Dedication to scientific advancement
  • Teacher approval and trust

Membership Requirements

1

Academic Excellence

Maintain at least a 3.75 weighted GPA overall with consistent high performance across all subjects

2

Science Grades

Achieve all A's or B's in every science class (rare exceptions may be considered)

3

Dual Credit Science

Enroll in at least one Dual Credit Science class during sophomore, junior, or senior year and remain enrolled for the full year

4

Teacher Approval

Receive approval from the majority of science teachers on campus during application review

Most Important Requirement

Students must exemplify the mission statement of the Science National Honor Society, demonstrating dedication to scientific knowledge that benefits all mankind.

Service Requirements for Membership

To remain a member, you must fulfill ALL FIVE service requirements:

1. Blood Drive Volunteer Service

Volunteer at all three blood drives during the school year (3 hours each):

  • Schedule blood donation appointments for applicants
  • Escort students from their classrooms to donation area
  • Ensure all donors arrive in a timely manner
  • Assist with coordination and logistics

2. School Science Fair Judge (December)

Volunteer as a judge at the school's science fair:

  • Event held on a Friday near the end of December
  • Judge freshmen science fair projects and posters
  • Attendance required even if field trip is scheduled
  • Demonstrate scientific evaluation skills

3. District Science Fair Judge (January)

Volunteer as a judge at the district science fair:

  • Provide your own transportation to venue
  • Location: Taylor/Hasting/Elsik High School or designated site
  • After school event lasting until approximately 6:00 PM
  • Evaluate district-level science projects

4. Meeting Attendance

Attend all SNHS meetings with proper communication:

  • Attendance at all scheduled meetings is mandatory
  • Absences must be prearranged with an officer or sponsor
  • Only legitimate conflicts will be considered
  • Active participation in society activities

5. Exemplify the Mission Statement

The most important requirement for continued membership:

  • Demonstrate commitment to scientific advancement
  • Show dedication to community service
  • Maintain high standards of character and integrity
  • Pursue scientific knowledge that benefits mankind

Application Process

Submit the following materials to Dr. Chen or Mr. Nguyen:

  • Unofficial Transcript: Include all science classes and grades highlighted
  • Grade Requirements: Must include freshmen, sophomore, and junior grades
  • GPA Highlighting: Have your overall GPA clearly highlighted
  • Important: Failure to highlight required information will result in automatic disqualification
  • Character Essay: Demonstrate your character, academic achievements, and commitment

Review Process & Membership Fee

Dr. Chen and Mr. Nguyen will carefully review all applicants based on character, grades, and essays. Accepted students will be notified through Remind and must pay a $20 non-refundable membership fee that includes your medal, shirt, and graduation cord.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I have to donate blood?

No, you do not have to donate blood to join. You just need to volunteer for the blood drive and science fair activities. Your role is to help coordinate and assist, not to donate blood yourself.

What is the benefit of joining Science National Honor Society?

It looks excellent on college transcripts because it tells universities and colleges that you have excellent character, grades, and shows that teachers trust you. That is exactly what universities and colleges look for in an applicant. If you are in Science National Honor Society, you basically tell schools you have what they are searching for. You also get a free t-shirt, medal, and graduation cord.

Is [insert class] considered a dual credit science class?

Here is the list of HCC classes that we accept: If it is not listed below, we will not count it! Classes from Social and Behavioral Sciences will not count.

Chemistry: CHEM 1305, 1405, 1411, 1412

Geography: GEOG 1301

Geology: GEOL 1305, 1345, 1347, 1403, 1404

Physics: PHYS 1305, 1401, 1402, 2325, 2125(Lab), 2326, 2126(Lab)

Life & Physical Sciences: ANTH 2301, ASTR 1303, 1304, 1403, 1404, BIOL 1308, 1309, 1322, 1406, 1407

How do I know if I got accepted or not?

We find you and let you know, most likely through Remind. If you are not contacted, that means you were not accepted.

What criteria do you look for?

Besides the grades, we (all four science teachers) look at your character as a person:

• Have you been excessively tardy? (This includes being late to study hall!)
• Are you respectful to your peers and teachers?
• Are you mature enough to handle failure?
• Did you receive an office referral?
• Do you work in the classroom?
• Are you paying attention in class?
• Are you the kind of person who leads by example instead of following people?

Since SNHS runs the blood drives entirely on their own (and we have yet to fail to meet quota), we only choose members that we have 100% trust in. If an issue comes up, you will resolve it. If you give any reason for us not to trust you with this responsibility, then we will not select you.

Are the decisions final?

Yes, they are. We go through the applications twice to confirm our decisions.

Can I be removed once I am accepted?

Yes, you can. If you have a disciplinary issue (suspension) or miss a meeting for no reason, then we will remove you.
